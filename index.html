About me

I am a second-year PhD student in Human-Computer Interaction at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou), advised by Prof. Mingming Fan. My research focuses on companion robots for older adults, with a particular emphasis on nonverbal behavior design — how a robot's gaze, gesture, and expressive movement shape perceptions of companionship, trust, and care.

Before starting my PhD, I spent nearly a decade working across innovation, user research, and product management at Siemens, Bosch Siemens Home Appliances, and China Mobile, leading user research for products used by millions of consumers and directing 0-to-1 product definition end to end. That experience shapes how I approach research today: grounded in real user needs, rigorous in method, and oriented toward design implications that matter.

Research Interests

  • Nonverbal Behavior Design

    Designing robot gaze, gesture, and expressive motion to communicate intent and emotion.

  • Human-Robot Interaction

    Understanding how people perceive, trust, and relate to social and companion robots.

  • Companion Robots & Aging

    Exploring older adults' needs, expectations, and role positioning for companion robots.

  • Mixed-Methods HCI Research

    Combining qualitative and quantitative user research with design thinking and co-design.
